  • How to not get caught!
    1. Don't look suspicious. It's important to strike a balance between effectively getting your answers and not making it obvious.
    2. If you must look around, never hold your gaze in one place for longer than five to ten seconds.
    3. Don't aim too high. If you cheat your way into a perfect score, while everyone else is getting an unremarkable grade on the exam, you will draw attention to yourself.
    4. Dispose of the evidence. As soon as the test is over, ask to go to the bathroom (if you haven't already gone) to wash off or throw away any evidence of cheating.
    5. Always be aware of where the teacher is looking. No method of cheating is successful if the teacher is looking right at you while you frantically copy off of your cheatsheet.
    6. Write or print the information correctly. The font should always be clear to read (not too big, not too small; size 8 is usually the smallest font people can read).
    7. Partnering is always better than cheat sheets and the hard-to-prove methods are even better. Overall, the less evidence there is - the better it is for you.
    8. Don't brag about it. This may seem obvious, but it is something people do. You don't know who could tell the teacher.
